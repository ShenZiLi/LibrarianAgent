# Shanghai Transport

Shanghai boasts one of the worlds most extensive public transit networks. The metro system operates nineteen lines covering over eight hundred kilometers, connecting Pudong Airport to the city center in under forty-five minutes via the Maglev train, which reaches four hundred thirty-one kilometers per hour. The bus network complements rail with over one thousand routes, including night buses running until midnight. Ride-hailing apps dominate daily commuting, with Didi Chuxing offering everything from economy sedans to premium electric vehicles. Shared bicycles from Meituan and HelloBike fill the last-mile gap, scattered at virtually every metro exit. The Huangpu River ferry provides a scenic crossing between Puxi and Pudong for just two yuan. For intercity travel, Hongqiao Railway Station connects to Beijing in four hours by high-speed rail, while Pudong International Airport serves over seventy million passengers annually with flights to every continent.
