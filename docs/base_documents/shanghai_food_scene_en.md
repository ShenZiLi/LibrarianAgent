# Shanghai Food Scene

Shanghai is a paradise for food lovers, blending regional Chinese cuisines with international flavors. The iconic Xiaolongbao soup dumpling originated here, with Jia Jia Tang Bao on Huanghe Road serving some of the finest specimens. Yunnan South Road Food Street offers a concentrated taste of local snacks, from scallion oil noodles to pan-fried pork buns. The French Concession district hides countless bistros and craft cocktail bars behind plane tree canopies. For upscale dining, Three Michelin-starred Ultraviolet by Paul Pairet delivers a multi-sensory tasting menu unlike anywhere else on earth. The city also embraces global cuisine, with authentic ramen in Gubei, Korean barbecue in Hongqiao, and Italian trattorias along the Bund. Street vendors selling jianbing crepes and stinky tofu can still be found near temple markets, preserving the citys humble culinary roots.
