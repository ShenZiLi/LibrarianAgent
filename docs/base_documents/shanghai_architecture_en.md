# Shanghai Architecture

Shanghais skyline tells the story of a city perpetually reinventing itself. The Bunds neoclassical banking halls from the 1920s face off against Pudongs futuristic trio: the Oriental Pearl Towers space-age spheres, the Jin Mao Towers stepped pagoda form, and the Shanghai Towers spiraling glass cylinder reaching six hundred thirty-two meters. The city pioneered Art Deco in Asia, with the Park Hotel and the Grand Theatre standing as gilded monuments to the jazz age. Contemporary architects have left bold marks too: David Chipperfields Rockbund Art Museum carved a modern gallery from a 1930s library, while Heatherwick Studios 1000 Trees mall erupts with concrete columns topped with greenery like a terraced hillside. In the old lanes, shikumen townhouses with stone gateframes and black lacquered doors are being adaptively reused as boutique hotels and creative offices, proving that preservation and progress can coexist in this ever-changing metropolis.
